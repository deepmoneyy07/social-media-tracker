# Vantage Circle Social Media Copywriting Skill

Master social media copywriting across all platforms with platform-specific strategies, templates, and frameworks. Generate high-converting posts, captions, and scripts that balance lead generation, thought leadership, and community engagement.

## 1. Brand Voice by Platform

### LinkedIn
**Tone:** Professional, data-driven, authoritative
**Goal:** Lead generation + thought leadership
**Audience:** HR leaders, executives, decision-makers
**Length:** Medium (150-300 words typical)
**Style:** Problem → Insight → Solution (with subtle CTA)
**Authenticity:** Share research, client wins (anonymized), industry trends

### Reddit
**Tone:** Conversational, authentic, expert-helpful
**Goal:** Community engagement + authority building
**Audience:** HR practitioners, engaged community members
**Length:** Long-form (500-1000+ words acceptable)
**Style:** Question/Problem → Deep Answer → Natural mention
**Authenticity:** Never promotional; solve genuine problems first

### Instagram
**Tone:** Inspirational, visual-first, culture-focused
**Goal:** Brand awareness + employee advocacy
**Audience:** Employees, HR professionals, culture enthusiasts
**Length:** Short (caption 100-150 words)
**Style:** Emotional hook → Visual + Insight → Call to action
**Authenticity:** Employee stories, behind-the-scenes, culture moments

### Twitter/X
**Tone:** Sharp, opinionated, thought-leadership hot takes
**Goal:** Thought leadership + viral reach
**Audience:** HR tech community, founders, early adopters
**Length:** Short (280 characters sweet spot, up to 1000 acceptable)
**Style:** Bold statement → Why it matters → Optional link
**Authenticity:** Takes, observations, rapid-fire insights

### Email/Blog (Supporting)
**Tone:** Educational, thorough, actionable
**Goal:** Lead nurturing + SEO authority
**Audience:** People already in your funnel
**Length:** Long-form (1000-3000 words)
**Style:** Deep problem solve with framework + CTA
**Authenticity:** Research-backed, comprehensive guides

## 2. Core Copywriting Frameworks

### CRITICAL PRINCIPLE
**"If your posts aren't pulling the right people, the issue probably isn't value. It's your structure." — Daniel Korenblum, LinkedIn Marketing Insider**

Structure > Content Quality. A mediocre insight with perfect structure outperforms a brilliant insight with poor structure.

### Framework 1: AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action) - LinkedIn Authority Posts

**When to use:** Building thought leadership, establishing expertise, grabbing attention

**Structure:**
```
ATTENTION (Hook in first 1-2 lines):
- Bold, surprising statement OR contrarian take
- Should make someone stop scrolling
- Example: "Most recognition programs fail before they launch"

INTEREST (Why they should care):
- Connect to THEIR goals, not your product
- Show stakes: "Here's what happens if you don't fix this"
- Make it relevant to their situation
- 2-3 sentences max

DESIRE (Paint the outcome):
- Show what changes when it's solved
- Be specific: What does success look like?
- Make them want it
- Emotional + practical

ACTION (Tell them exactly what to do):
- Clear next step: "Comment below," "DM me," "Reply with..."
- Make it frictionless (not "Buy now")
- Specific, not vague
```

**LinkedIn Example for Vantage Circle:**

```
ATTENTION:
"Your recognition program is failing because you're measuring the wrong thing."

INTEREST:
"Most companies track adoption rate. They launch, hit 20-30%, and call it a win. But here's the real problem: 20% adoption looks like a program failure, not a culture shift. And your team feels it."

DESIRE:
"Imagine if 60%+ of your team was recognizing each other weekly. Not because they had to. Because they wanted to. That's when recognition stops being 'an HR thing' and becomes 'how we work here.' That's culture shift."

ACTION:
"What's been your biggest adoption blocker? Reply below—I'll share what's worked for similar companies."
```

**Why AIDA works:**
- Stops the scroll (Attention)
- Makes it relevant (Interest)
- Shows the dream (Desire)
- Low friction CTA (Action)

### Framework 2: BAB (Before → After → Bridge) - LinkedIn/Instagram Transformation Posts

**When to use:** Showing transformation, culture change, process improvement

**Structure:**
```
BEFORE (Paint the problem):
- Show what's NOT working
- Be specific: "We had 15% adoption"
- Make it relatable: "Sound familiar?"

AFTER (Paint the dream):
- Show the better outcome
- Be aspirational but believable
- "Imagine a team where..."
- Use sensory language (feels, looks, sounds)

BRIDGE (How to get there):
- The specific steps or approach
- This is where Vantage Circle's approach comes in
- Keep it simple: 1-3 steps
- NOT: "Use our product" BUT: "Start with peer recognition, not top-down"
```

**LinkedIn Example for Vantage Circle:**

```
BEFORE:
"Our recognition program launch was a disaster. We rolled out a tool, sent an email, and... nothing. Adoption stalled at 18%. We invested months and it felt like a failure."

AFTER:
"Six months later, it's completely different. Peer recognition is everywhere. People are genuinely appreciating each other. We can feel the culture shift. Retention improved 18%, and engagement scores jumped 25 points."

BRIDGE:
"Here's what changed: We stopped trying to mandate recognition from the top. Instead, we:
1. Made peer-to-peer the default (not top-down)
2. Tied every recognition to our core values
3. Made it mobile-first (frictionless)

That's it. Structure, not complexity."
```

**Why BAB works:**
- Current state is relatable (Before)
- Future state is aspirational (After)
- Path is clear (Bridge)
- Emotional + practical

### Framework 3: PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution) - Reddit/Deep-Dive Posts

**When to use:** Authority building, deep problem-solving, Reddit threads

**Structure:**
```
PROBLEM (State it clearly):
- What's broken or not working?
- Be specific, not vague
- "Recognition adoption is stuck at 20%"

AGITATION (Why it's worse than people think):
- Dig deeper into consequences
- "It's not just low adoption—it signals your culture isn't aligned"
- Paint the ripple effects
- This is often overlooked and makes PAS powerful
- 2-3 sentences showing the downstream impact

SOLUTION (Offer the fix):
- Clear, actionable path
- "Here's what works..."
- Share the framework or approach
- Don't hold back (generosity builds trust)
```

**Reddit Example for Vantage Circle:**

```
PROBLEM:
"Recognition program adoption plateaus at 20% in most companies. It's predictable, frustrating, and companies abandon the initiative after 6 months."

AGITATION:
"But here's what's actually happening: When adoption is low, people notice. Your team sees leadership using the tool but peers don't. It signals that recognition isn't actually part of your culture—it's an HR initiative. That's worse than not having a program at all. Because now your culture feels forced."

SOLUTION:
"Here's what I've seen work: Don't launch top-down. Launch peer-to-peer from day one.
1. Get 10-15 early adopters to recognize each other first
2. Let it spread naturally (peer-to-peer is viral)
3. Tie every recognition to your values (not performance)
4. Mobile-first (if it takes >30 seconds, adoption dies)

When you do this right, adoption hits 50-60% in 60-90 days. Because it feels authentic, not corporate."
```

**Why PAS works:**
- Problem statement is relatable (hook)
- Agitation deepens urgency (emotional resonance)
- Solution shows you understand the root cause (credibility)

### Framework 4: PAPA (Problem → Advantage → Proof → Action) - LinkedIn Lead-Gen Posts

**When to use:** Generating leads, demonstrating superiority, case-backed claims

**Structure:**
```
PROBLEM (What's not working):
- State the challenge clearly
- "Your approach isn't getting results"

ADVANTAGE (Why your way is better):
- What's different about your approach?
- Focus on the differentiator, not the product
- "Peer-to-peer adoption is 3x higher than top-down"

PROOF (Back it up with real results):
- Case study, metric, or example
- Anonymize but be specific
- "Mid-market tech company: 40% adoption in 60 days"
- This is the trust-builder

ACTION (Simple, clear next step):
- Low friction: "Reply," "DM," "Comment"
- Specific: "Reply with your biggest adoption challenge"
- NOT: "Schedule a demo" but "Let's discuss your situation"
```

**LinkedIn Example for Vantage Circle:**

```
PROBLEM:
"Your recognition program adoption is stuck because you're using the wrong model."

ADVANTAGE:
"Top-down recognition feels corporate. Peer-to-peer recognition feels genuine. When people can recognize each other, adoption jumps 3x."

PROOF:
"We worked with a 200-person tech company. Moved from top-down to peer-to-peer. Hit 60% adoption in 90 days. Retention improved 18%. Engagement scores jumped 22 points."

ACTION:
"What's your current adoption rate? Reply below—I'll share the specific framework that worked for them."
```

**Why PAPA works:**
- Problem establishes relevance (hook)
- Advantage differentiates (why us, not competitors)
- Proof removes doubt (social proof is powerful)
- Action is frictionless (low barrier)

## 3. Platform-Specific Copy Templates

### LinkedIn: Authority Post (AIDA Framework - Recognition Adoption)

**Template:**

```
HEADLINE:
"Why Your Recognition Program Adoption Is Stuck at 20% (And How to 3x It in 90 Days)"

BODY:
We just analyzed 200+ companies rolling out recognition programs. The pattern is frustratingly consistent:

Month 1: Excitement. "This is going to transform our culture!"
Month 3: Reality. 15-20% adoption. Managers aren't nominating. Employees aren't engaging.
Month 6: Abandonment. It's another failed HR initiative.

Here's what we found: The problem isn't the program. It's the launch.

Companies that hit 60%+ adoption within 90 days do three things differently:

1. **They start peer-to-peer, not top-down.** Top-down recognition feels corporate. Peer recognition feels genuine. When employees can recognize each other, adoption jumps 3x.

2. **They tie it to values, not performance.** "Recognize someone who lived our values this week" is infinitely better than "Recognize your best performer." Values are memorable. Performance is subjective.

3. **They make it frictionless.** If recognition takes more than 30 seconds, you lose 70% of potential recognizers. Mobile-first, one-click nominations, async options.

We've seen this work consistently: Mid-market tech company → 40% adoption in 30 days. Insurance firm → 65% adoption in 60 days. Non-profit → 55% adoption in 45 days.

The difference? Intentional launch strategy, not just rolling out a tool.

**What's been your biggest adoption blocker? Reply below—I'm happy to share the specific tactics we've seen work.**

[Optional: Link to resource or case study]
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with surprising claim (20% stuck)
- Validates reader experience (they've probably seen this)
- Shares framework (3 concrete principles)
- Proves it works (anonymized case studies)
- Ends with question (drives engagement, not a hard sell)
- No product name-drop (but Vantage Circle's approach is obvious)

### Reddit: Authority Post (Recognition Authenticity)

**Template:**

```
TITLE:
"Why peer recognition works (and top-down recognition doesn't) — breaking down the psychology"

BODY:
I've spent the last 5 years helping companies build recognition cultures, and I keep seeing the same pattern: programs that feel forced fail. Programs that feel authentic thrive.

Here's the psychological difference:

**Top-down recognition** ("Great job, team!"):
- Feels obligatory to both giver and receiver
- Manager probably has limited bandwidth; recognition feels generic
- Employees wonder: "Is this genuine or HR theater?"
- Result: Polite acceptance, not emotional resonance

**Peer recognition** ("Sarah, thank you for unblocking our project"):
- Feels voluntary and specific
- The giver noticed something real and took time to acknowledge it
- The receiver feels genuinely seen by their peer
- Result: Motivation, belonging, real cultural shift

I've watched companies move from model #1 to model #2, and the difference is night and day. Adoption goes from 20% to 60%+. Employees actually *want* to give recognition.

**Why peer recognition works:**
1. It's specific. Peers see the details managers miss.
2. It's voluntary. No manager mandate, so it feels authentic.
3. It's frequent. Peer recognition happens continuously, not quarterly.
4. It spreads culture. When you see your colleagues recognizing values-based behavior, you internalize what your company actually cares about.

**The catch:** You have to make it dead simple. If recognition takes more than 30 seconds, adoption tanks. Mobile-first, one-click, async—these are table stakes.

**What's been your experience?** Have you seen recognition programs that actually stuck? What made the difference?

[Don't mention Vantage Circle unless someone asks directly]
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with credibility (5 years experience)
- Psychological depth (not just "peer is better")
- Contrasts two approaches (makes the choice obvious)
- Gives readers a mental model they can use
- Invites engagement authentically (doesn't ask "want to try ours?")
- Solves genuine problem (not promotional)

### Framework 6: Video Reel Script (Problem/Solution Structure - Instagram/TikTok)

**When to use:** Short-form video, Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, thought leadership

**Critical Rules for Video Scripts:**
- **Visual change every 3-5 seconds** (or people stop watching)
- **Sound on by default** (captions essential, voiceover secondary)
- **First 2 seconds are EVERYTHING** (hook before they swipe)
- **Text overlays > voiceover** (most people watch muted)
- **Length: 15-45 seconds** (sweet spot on Instagram is 21-34 seconds)

**Structure:**

```
[SECONDS 0-3] HOOK (Stop the scroll)
Visual: Text overlay, change, or surprising image
Text: Bold claim or question
Voiceover: Optional but punchy ("This is backwards...")
Goal: Make them stop swiping

[SECONDS 3-8] PROBLEM (Make it relatable)
Visual: Show the pain point
Text: What's wrong
Voiceover: "Most companies do X..."
Goal: "Yes, that's me!" moment

[SECONDS 8-15] INSIGHT/REFRAME (The aha moment)
Visual: Show the shift or reveal
Text: The key insight
Voiceover: "But here's what actually matters..."
Goal: Make them think differently

[SECONDS 15-21] SOLUTION (Show the better way)
Visual: Show the right approach
Text: Key principle or framework
Voiceover: "Instead, try X..."
Goal: Practical, actionable

[SECONDS 21-30] PROOF/OUTCOME (Build credibility)
Visual: Result, case study, or happy outcome
Text: Metric or result
Voiceover: "We saw X improve by Y%"
Goal: Make it believable

[SECONDS 30-34] CTA (Low friction ask)
Visual: Back to camera or text
Text: "Comment," "DM," "Follow"
Voiceover: Optional
Goal: Easy engagement
```

**Instagram Reel Example for Vantage Circle:**

```
[0-3] HOOK
Text overlay: "Your recognition program is failing"
Visual: Frustrated HR manager at desk
Voiceover: "And you don't even know why"

[3-8] PROBLEM
Text overlay: "20% adoption rate"
Visual: Team ignoring recognition feature
Voiceover: "You launch. Adoption stalls. People don't engage."

[8-15] REFRAME
Text overlay: "The problem isn't the program..."
Visual: Change to peer-to-peer recognition

[15-21] SOLUTION
Text overlay: "3-part framework:"
1. "Peer-to-peer first (not top-down)"
2. "Tie to values (not performance)"
3. "Mobile-first (frictionless)"
Visual: Show each principle with graphics
Voiceover: "When you do this, adoption jumps to 60%"

[21-30] PROOF
Text overlay: "Real result:"
"200-person tech company"
"20% → 60% adoption in 90 days"
"18% retention improvement"
Visual: Simple metrics graphic
Voiceover: "We've seen this work consistently"

[30-34] CTA
Text overlay: "What's YOUR adoption rate?"

Voiceover: Optional
```

**Why this reel structure works:**
- Stops the scroll immediately (hook)
- Problem is specific and relatable (recognition)
- Reframe shows there's a better way (insight)
- Solution is concrete and actionable (3 steps)
- Proof removes skepticism (real metrics)
- CTA is frictionless (just comment)

**Video Script Best Practices:**

**Do:**
- Change visuals every 3-5 seconds (pacing)
- Use text overlays for key points (captions)
- Lead with voiceover OR text, not both
- Include captions for ALL voiceover (muted viewing)
- End with clear, low-friction CTA
- Use pattern interrupts (colors, cuts, transitions)
- Include music/sound design (sets tone)

**Don't:**
- Static shots longer than 5 seconds (boring)
- Voiceover without text (accessibility issue)
- Weak CTAs ("Check us out") — be specific
- Slow pacing — edit tight
- Complex information (video can't handle it)
- Salesy tone — educational > promotional
- Forget captions (80% watch muted)

## 4. Best Practices Checklists

### Before You Post

- [ ] **Authenticity test:** Could I say this in a real conversation with an HR leader?
- [ ] **Problem-first test:** Does this solve a genuine problem or just promote Vantage Circle?
- [ ] **Platform tone test:** Does this match the platform's expected voice?
- [ ] **Credibility test:** Is this backed by data, experience, or genuine insight?
- [ ] **Engagement test:** Does this invite conversation or just broadcast?
- [ ] **Value test:** Would someone reshare/retweet this for its insight, not just to help Vantage Circle?

### Post Timing & Cadence

**LinkedIn:**
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 8am-12pm EST (office browsing hours)
- **Cadence:** 1-2 posts per week (quality over frequency)
- **Engagement window:** First 1 hour critical—respond to comments

**Reddit:**
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 9am-2pm EST (community active)
- **Cadence:** 1-2 high-value posts per week MAX (oversaturation kills credibility)
- **Engagement window:** First 2 hours critical—comment on every top reply

**Instagram:**
- **Best times:** Mon, Wed, Fri 10am-2pm EST
- **Cadence:** 2-3 posts per week (daily Stories okay)
- **Engagement window:** First 4 hours (stories expire in 24h)

**Twitter:**
- **Best times:** Continuous—multiple times daily if relevant
- **Cadence:** 1-5 tweets per day (threads > individual tweets)
- **Engagement window:** First 30 minutes critical for algorithm

### Comment/Reply Strategy

**LinkedIn:**
- Reply to 100% of comments in first hour
- Ask follow-up questions (deepen conversation)
- Don't hard-sell—be generous with insights
- If someone mentions a problem → "I've seen this solved 3 ways..."

**Reddit:**
- Reply to 100% of comments first 2 hours
- Engage respectfully with criticism (don't delete disagreement)
- Share frameworks and insights freely
- Only mention Vantage Circle if commenter asks directly
- Flag high-intent comments for lead qualification

**Instagram:**
- Reply to 80%+ of comments first 4 hours
- Use emojis and conversational language
- Ask follow-up questions to drive engagement
- If someone asks about solutions → DM them link

**Twitter:**
- Reply to quote-tweets and critical replies
- Engage in threads (don't just promote your own)
- Retweet quality comments with your take
- Build community, not just audience

## 11. Framework Selection Guide

**Which Framework to Use (Quick Reference):**

| Goal | Framework | Best Platform | Why |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **Grab attention + convert** | AIDA | LinkedIn, Instagram | Full funnel: hook → desire → action |
| **Show transformation** | BAB | LinkedIn, Instagram | Emotional: before/after resonates |
| **Deep authority + expertise** | PAS | Reddit, Blog | Problem diving shows mastery |
| **Generate leads** | PAPA | LinkedIn | Proof + advantage = credibility |
| **Viral short-form** | Video Hook (3-5 sec rule) | Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts | Pacing + visuals > words |
| **Complex ideas** | Carousel | LinkedIn | 10-slide structure handles nuance |
| **Quick takes** | Hot Take (bold + why) | Twitter/X | Opinionated + shareable |

**Framework Combinations That Work:**

- **PAPA + Video Script** = LinkedIn post + Reel (lead magnet + awareness)
- **BAB + Carousel** = Transformation story across 10 slides
- **PAS + Reddit + Long-form** = Deep authority in communities
- **AIDA + Carousel** = Hook on Slide 1, then full AIDA across remaining slides

## 12. What Works vs. What Doesn't (From Analysis)

### Structure Matters More Than Content

**DOESN'T WORK:**
- "Sharing value" without structure ("Here's 5 tips for recognition")
- Generic advice without specific problem ("Recognition is important!")
- All text, no visual hierarchy
- Multiple ideas crammed together
- Weak CTAs ("Follow for more")

**WORKS:**
- Specific problem + specific solution (AIDA, PAS, PAPA)
- One clear idea per post
- Visual hierarchy: bold statement → supporting detail → CTA
- Clear hook in first 2 seconds (video) or first 1-2 lines (text)
- Strong, specific CTAs ("Reply with your adoption rate")

---

### LinkedIn: What Actually Gets Engagement

**DOESN'T WORK:**
❌ "Hey everyone, check out this insight about recognition"
❌ "5 tips to improve culture" (generic)
❌ Long wall of text (no formatting)
❌ "If you like this, follow for more"
❌ Product pitch disguised as insight

**WORKS:**
✅ "Your recognition program failed because you're measuring the wrong thing"
✅ Specific problem + specific framework (AIDA structure)
✅ Visual formatting: short paragraphs, bold key phrases
✅ "Reply with your adoption rate—I'll share what worked for similar companies"
✅ Solve the problem first, mention Vantage Circle naturally if relevant

---

### Instagram/Reels: Visual Rules That Drive Views

**DOESN'T WORK:**
❌ Static shot longer than 5 seconds
❌ Voiceover with no captions (accessibility fail)
❌ Text too small (unreadable at scroll speed)
❌ Slow pacing / long intro
❌ Weak hook (first 2 seconds don't grab attention)

**WORKS:**
✅ Visual change every 3-5 seconds
✅ Text overlays for all voiceovers (captions)
✅ Bold text, large fonts, high contrast
✅ Fast-paced, immediate value
✅ Hook in first 2 seconds (problem statement or surprising stat)

---

### Reddit: Authenticity Rules

**DOESN'T WORK:**
❌ "Check out Vantage Circle's recognition solution"
❌ Generic advice (everyone already knows this)
❌ Long post that feels like a sales page
❌ Not answering the actual question people asked
❌ Editing to remove any mention of competition

**WORKS:**
✅ PAS framework: deep problem → why it matters → clear solution
✅ Specific, hard-won insights ("I've seen this 200+ times")
✅ Sharing frameworks freely (generosity > promotion)
✅ Directly answering the question, then offering framework
✅ Acknowledging other solutions exist ("Competitors do X, but...")

---

### Twitter/X: Hot Takes That Spread

**DOESN'T WORK:**
❌ "Recognition programs help with retention 📈"
❌ Agreeable takes (everyone already thinks this)
❌ Links in every tweet
❌ "My thoughts on HR tech..."
❌ No call for engagement

**WORKS:**
✅ "Your recognition program failed because you're measuring the wrong metric"
✅ Slightly contrarian but backed up
✅ Link sparingly (value first, link second)
✅ Strong POV stated as fact
✅ "What's your take?" or "Agree or disagree?"

---

### Video Reels: The 3-5 Second Rule

**DOESN'T WORK:**
❌ Talking head for 20 seconds
❌ One static graphic
❌ No captions (80% watch muted)
❌ Expecting viewers to read long text
❌ Slow reveals or buildup

**WORKS:**
✅ Visual change every 3-5 seconds
✅ Motion, cuts, transitions keeping pace up
✅ Captions for ALL content
✅ Text + visual + motion aligned
✅ Hook in first 2 seconds (problem state or stat)

## 13. Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Being too promotional** — "Check out Vantage Circle!" (People hate this)
✅ **Solving the problem first** — "Here's the framework" (mention VC naturally or not at all)

❌ **Generic content** — "Recognition is important!" (Boring, no engagement)
✅ **Specific insights** — "We analyzed 200 companies and found X pattern" (Credible, shareable)

❌ **Platform mismatch** — LinkedIn tone on Reddit (Communities have different cultures)
✅ **Platform-native voice** — Professional on LinkedIn, authentic on Reddit (Respectful of norms)

❌ **Asking for the sale too early** — "Want to demo Vantage Circle?" (Kills trust)
✅ **Building authority first** — Share insights, let them come to you (Better conversion)

❌ **Posting inconsistently** — Random posts whenever you remember (Algorithms don't favor this)
✅ **Consistent cadence** — Same times, predictable value (Followers anticipate your content)

❌ **Ignoring comments** — Post and disappear (Engagement dies)
✅ **Active engagement** — Reply thoughtfully within the first hour (Algorithms boost active posts)
